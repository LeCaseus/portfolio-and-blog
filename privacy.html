what's collected, why, and on what legal basis

Under GDPR, every category of personal data processed needs a stated legal basis. Here's each one this site actually touches:

  • Analytics (self-hosted Umami): page URL, referrer, browser/device type, and an approximate country derived from your IP address at request time. No cookies, no persistent visitor ID, no cross-site tracking. Legal basis: legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) — understanding basic traffic to a personal site, weighed against your privacy since no individual-level profile is built or retained.
  • Reactions (♥ button on posts): your IP address is received and immediately hashed (SHA-256) before storage; only the hash is kept, tied to the post slug, to prevent duplicate reactions. Legal basis: legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) — abuse prevention on a public feature, using the least identifying method available (a one-way hash).
  • Theme preference: stored only in your browser's localStorage. Never transmitted to this site's server. Legal basis: not applicable — this isn't processing by me as controller, since the data never reaches me.
  • Infrastructure/security logs (Cloudflare, CrowdSec): IP address and standard request metadata (user agent, timestamps, request paths), logged transiently for routing, DDoS mitigation, and abuse/intrusion detection. Legal basis: legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) — securing the service, a recognized legitimate interest under Recital 49.

None of the above is used for advertising, profiling, or automated decision-making with legal or similarly significant effects on you.

how long data is kept

  • Analytics data: retained indefinitely on my own server. There is no automatic expiry — data is only removed if I manually delete it from the Umami database.
  • Reaction hashes: retained indefinitely, tied to the post slug, until you request deletion or I manually clear the reaction_log table.
  • Infrastructure/security logs: retention is set by Cloudflare and CrowdSec's own configurations, not by me directly. See their respective policies for specifics.

Indefinite retention is a deliberate choice, not an oversight — post statistics are only useful to me if they're continuous rather than reset on a schedule. This is disclosed here so it's explicit rather than left as "as long as necessary."

who else sees data, and where it goes

No data is sold, rented, or shared for marketing purposes, with anyone, ever.

The following third parties process data on this site's behalf purely as infrastructure — not as recipients who use your data for their own purposes:

  • Cloudflare — CDN, DNS, tunnel, and reverse proxy in front of the origin server. Cloudflare is a global network, meaning your request may be routed through servers outside your country, including outside the EU/EEA. See Cloudflare's privacy policy for their safeguards on international transfers.
  • CrowdSec — self-hosted intrusion/abuse prevention running alongside the origin server, used to identify and block malicious traffic patterns.
  • Webring embeds (site footer): some webring widgets on this site load a small script or banner directly from the ring's own server (e.g. softmachine.dev, webring.antaresph.dev). These third-party embeds outside my control — the ring provider may set their own cookies or log your visit independently of anything described in this policy. Legal basis: not applicable — I am not the controller for data these embeds collect; see each ring's own privacy policy.

Analytics (Umami) and the reactions database run entirely on hardware I own — that data does not leave my own infrastructure.

your rights

If GDPR applies to you (generally, if you're in the EU/EEA/UK), you have the right to:

  • Access — ask what data, if any, is held about you.
  • Rectification — ask for inaccurate data to be corrected.
  • Erasure — ask for your data (e.g. a reaction hash) to be deleted.
  • Restriction — ask for processing of your data to be limited.
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Portability — request a copy of data you've provided, in a portable format (limited applicability here, since almost nothing identifiable is stored).
  • Lodge a complaint — with your national data protection supervisory authority, if you believe your data has been mishandled. (In the EU, find yours via the EDPB member list.)

To exercise any of these, email cheztervargas353@gmail.com. Because almost nothing on this site identifies you directly, most requests will amount to confirming no identifiable record exists, or deleting a reaction hash tied to your IP.

cookies

This site does not set any cookies. The only client-side storage is localStorage for your theme preference, which is not a cookie, isn't sent to any server, and isn't used for tracking.

Some embedded third-party widgets (see "who else sees data") may set their own cookies independently of this site.

changes to this policy

If what this site collects or how it's processed changes, this page will be updated and the "effective" date at the top will change accordingly. Material changes affecting your rights will be reflected here before they take effect — there's no separate mailing list to notify, since this site doesn't collect emails.
